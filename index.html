Chicken for All Seasons

I prepare this when I need something easy and tasty for my family.
  • one tray of chicken legs, about 4-6 pounds
  • Morton's Season-All
  1. Place chicken legs in a single layer on a shallow baking pan and sprinkle with Season-All to taste.
  2. Bake in a 350-degree oven for one hour.
Yields 6-8 servings.

Chicken Stew

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 medium potatoes
  • 6 medium carrots
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 tbsp. sugar
  • 3 tbsp. soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp. sesame oil
  • garlic powder to taste
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • ground red pepper to taste
  • 5-8 shiitake mushrooms (optional)
  1. If using dried mushrooms, soak them in warm water for 20 minutes or until soft. Cut off stems and slice caps into 1/4- to 1/2-inch wide strips.
  2. Put all ingredients into a large pot and stir to combine. Cover and bring to a gentle boil over medium-high heat, and then decrease heat to low. Simmer for one hour, stirring once or twice.
  3. Serve with rice and other side vegetables.
Yields 6-8 servings.

Sunshine Chicken

  • one tray of chicken legs, about 4-6 pounds
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced or garlic powder to taste
  • 1/2 c. soy sauce
  • 1/2 c. ketchup
  • 1/4 c. canola or corn oil
  • 1/4 c. honey
  • 1 tsp. dried basil
  • 1/4 tsp. ground black pepper
  1. Combine all ingredients except chicken in a large bowl and stir to combine.
  2. Add chicken to bowl and combine so that chicken is co with sauce. Marinate for up to a day in refrigerator.
  3. Place chicken in a single layer in a shallow baking pan and baste with sauce. Bake uncovered in a 350-degree oven for one hour.
For less fat you can remove the skin of the chicken before adding it to the marinade and it still comes out delicious. Yields 6-8 servings.
